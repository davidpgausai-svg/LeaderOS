# **3. Core Fields ‚Äî Enterprise Definitions & Guidance**

## **A. Mission**

A durable, time-agnostic statement that clarifies **why the enterprise exists** and the value it brings.
Best practice: Keep it concise (1‚Äì2 lines).
Used as: The north star for all decisions.

---

## **B. Vision**

A directional, aspirational statement that describes **where the organization must be in 3‚Äì5 years**.
Used as: The anchor for prioritization and transformation.

---

## **C. Strategic Priorities (3‚Äì5)**

These are the **enterprise-level pillars** that describe *what we must get right* over the next 12‚Äì36 months to fulfill the vision.

Characteristics:

* Non-overlapping
* Actionable yet broad enough to house multiple initiatives
* Durable across planning cycles
* Resource allocation follows these pillars

Examples:

* Workforce & Culture
* Digital Excellence
* Operational Efficiency
* Clinical Quality & Safety
* Financial Sustainability

You will eventually tie:

* Projects ‚Üí to a priority
* KPIs ‚Üí to a priority
* Accountability ‚Üí to a priority owner

---

## **D. Objectives (Under Each Priority)**

Objectives articulate the **meaningful, time-bound change** we intend to create under each priority.

Characteristics:

* Qualitative
* Directional
* Time-bound (12‚Äì18 months)
* Wholly owned by an accountable executive

Template:
**"Achieve ___ by ___ so that ___."**

Example:
"Reduce clinical onboarding time by 30% by FY26 so clinicians can practice faster and accelerate revenue capture."

---

## **E. Success Metrics / KPIs**

These quantify **how we measure progress** toward the objective.

Characteristics:

* Outcome-based
* Lagging + leading indicators
* Limited to 3‚Äì5 per objective (to maintain focus)
* Must be reportable without manual gymnastics
* Must be reviewed quarterly during enterprise scorecard cycles

Examples:

* Net Patient Revenue per Case Mix-Adjusted Discharge
* Clinician Time to Productivity
* Employee Engagement eNPS
* Cost per RVU
* Data Quality Score in EHR

**In your platform**, these metrics will feed:

* enterprise-level scoreboard
* dashboards
* quarterly business review decks

---

## **F. Key Initiatives**

Initiatives convert **intent ‚Üí execution**.
They are the *strategic investments, programs, or multi-step projects* required to achieve the objectives.

Characteristics:

* 8‚Äì20 per enterprise SOaP (depending on size)
* Multi-quarter, cross-functional, and resource-intensive
* Drive capital, technology, workforce, and operational constraints
* Require governance (RACI, risks, comms, sequencing)

Initiatives later break down into:

* Projects
* Actions
* Dependencies

---

## **G. Risks & Mitigations**

Strategic risk signals the **cracks in the foundation** that could compromise the plan.

Categories:

* Capacity and resourcing constraints
* Technology readiness / dependency risk
* Compliance & regulatory exposure
* Financial headwinds
* Cultural or change-adoption risk
* Vendor or partnership risk

Each risk includes:

1. Description
2. Impact
3. Likelihood
4. Mitigation Strategy
5. Contingency Trigger

This is what executives look at in quarterly strategic reviews.
